'Dur Drishti' - The Defence Management Journal has the unique distinction of being the only journal dedicated to management thought in the defence forces of India since 197 4. Through its continuous evolution, this journal has provided our strategic leaders with thought-provoking research on the most pressing issues in defence management. The growth in the depth and breadth of the topics covered mirrors the expanding challenges and opportunities foced by today's defence forces.

In a rapidly changing technological environment, the need to constantly reassess and adapt our approaches to defence management has never been greater. At CDM, we strive to keep pace with these developments by enhancing our academic outreach, regularly updating our curriculum, and incorporating emerging competencies such as Cyber Warfare, Space Logistics and Artificial Intelligence. These efforts ensure that our participants are equipped with the knowledge and skills necessary to address current and future challenges.

This issue of Dur Drishti brings together a remarkable array of articles, each contributing to the broader discourse on self-reliance and technological innovation in the defence sector. Themes of indigenous defence manufacturing and the role of Al in operational capabilities are the prime focus, given India's strategic intent on Atmanirbharta. The issue also delves into human-centric challenges, exploring work-life balance for women officers and inclusive education while drawing on global comparisons to enrich our understanding of defence management practices worldwide.

While excellent research standards and scholarly aptitude remain the hallmark of COM, these have been strengthened by impetus for quality and originality. The articles featured in this volume are drawn from a diverse pool of contributors, including distinguished alumni and external experts, ensuring a rich blend of perspectives. The high standards of research and originality in this edition reflect CDM's commitment to fostering intellectual rigor and challenging conventional wisdom. This journal remains a vital platform for critical debate and scholarly engagement, upholding the values that define CDM's academic community.

It is indeed a matter of immensely pride for me to bring out the second issue of Volume XXIII Issue 2 of 'Dur Drishti'. I extend my deepest appreciation to the editorial team for their dedication in curating this outstanding collection of articles. I encourage readers to engage with the ideas presented, as they provide valuable insights into the future of defence management. We welcome your feedback and contributions, which are essential to keeping Dur Drishti at the forefront of defence scholarship.
