From the Editorial Team

Dear Readers,

We are pleased to present the second issue of Vol XX:111 of the Defence Management Journal 'Dur Drishti'. We feel honoured to be a port of this peer-reviewed notional journal, which has made its mark as a trusted source in defence management. The submission of many articles from our scholars, alumni, and the environment is a clear testament that Dur Drishti continues to be a favoured port of coll for management and leadership thinkers.

This edition offers a rich tapestry of articles reflecting the dynamic landscape of defence management. The insights shared in these works underscore the evolving challenges and opportunities within the defence sector, particularly in technological advancements, policy reforms, and strategic self-reliance.

This issue's selection of papers brings together a variety of perspectives, exploring themes such as supply chain resilience, indigenous innovation, personnel weHore, and the integration of civil-military capacities. In particular, the emphasis on self-reliance in defence manufacturing, bolstered by lessons from other notions, resonates with the contemporary push for Atmanirbharta. Additionally, the role of artificial intelligence in transforming military logistics and the critical management strategies for developing indigenous aero-engines highlight the need for self-reliance in technology and strategic autonomy.

From a qualitative study on inclusive education in Army Public Schools to an analysis of workÂ­life balance among women officers in the Indian Armed Forces, this issue also highlights personnel challenges, addressing the all-important, 'man behind the machines'.

The issue also addresses lessons learned from defence industrial complexes in the developed countries. The article on the personnel welfare in the Nigerian Armed Forces brings in perspectives from our international alumni and is a valuable addition to the discourse on management. This diverse range of topics offers valuable insights into how different nations approach defence management, providing comparative frameworks that con inform India's strategies.

These papers contribute valuable knowledge with practical implications for policymakers and practitioners. Together, they reflect a holistic approach to advancing our understanding of defence management, fostering innovation, and ensuring the readiness and resilience of our defence forces.

The journal's continued success is the result of the labour of our contributors, who chose 'Dur Drishti' as the journal to publish their research, the unprejudiced efforts of peer reviewers, and the creative feedback of our subscribers. As always, we look forward to your suggestions, critiques, and thoughts on keeping 'Dur Drishti' the country's premier journal of defence management.
